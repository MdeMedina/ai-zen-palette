# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Mixed audience — executives (C-suite, founders), internal strategic advisors, and technical product teams. Users arrive with real cognitive load: a decision to make, a conflict to process, or a strategy to stress-test. They are not exploring; they are working. The interface must serve focused, intentional use — never interrupting, never decorating unnecessarily.

## Product Purpose

PKGD OS is an internal operating system for strategic thinking and decision-making. The Oracle module is a thinking partner — not a chatbot — for working through decisions. The Hive and Knowledge modules are operational tools, not exploratory surfaces. The Audit module supports review/oversight workflows. Success looks like: the user extracts clarity from complexity with minimal friction, and the interface never competes for attention with the thinking it's meant to support.

## Brand Personality

Three words: **Austero. Vivo. Preciso.** (Austere. Alive. Precise.)

A dark-first OS aesthetic with a single sharp accent (`#fae714`) that cuts through the dark. Institutional but not cold — it should feel like something that takes the user seriously. Emotional goal: presence and containment — the user should feel they are in a focused space where thinking is possible. No urgency, no visual noise; the silence is designed.

## Anti-references

- No glassmorphism for its own sake
- No gradient-heavy hero sections
- No playful rounded blobs
- No bright multi-color palettes
- Avoid anything that feels like a SaaS landing page or consumer app vying for attention
- Reference: Perplexity AI — clean chat interface, focused information hierarchy, no decorative chrome

## Design Principles

1. **Restraint as respect.** Empty space is where thinking happens. Never fill or decorate a screen just to avoid whitespace — especially for users carrying real cognitive load.
2. **Signal over decoration.** The accent color marks only what demands attention (active states, CTAs, live indicators, alerts). Using it elsewhere dilutes its meaning and erodes trust in the signal.
3. **Match the surface to its job.** Oracle is a thinking partner (conversational); Hive, Knowledge, and Audit are operational tools (efficient, task-focused). Don't apply exploratory/chat patterns to operational surfaces or vice versa.
4. **Containment over expansion.** Components should feel bounded and deliberate, like a precision instrument — not an open-ended canvas inviting exploration.
5. **One structural language, multiple states.** Dark mode (default), light mode, and the yellow/black "impact" mode for covers and major transitions all share the same structural grammar. A mode change signals a state change, not a different product.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA across the app. Body text ≥4.5:1 contrast, large text (≥18px or bold ≥14px) ≥3:1 — in both dark and light layout variants. Full keyboard navigation and screen-reader support expected, given the executive/operational user base relies on this as a working tool, not a casual surface. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
